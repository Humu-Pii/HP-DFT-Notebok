Welcome to our introduction to Density Functional Theory (DFT) simulation! DFT is a powerful computational method used in materials science, chemistry, and physics to analyze the electronic structure and properties of molecules, solids, and nanostructures. At its core, DFT simplifies the quantum mechanical problem by focusing on the electron density rather than directly solving the Schrödinger equation. This approach makes DFT highly versatile and efficient, with applications ranging from predicting material properties to studying chemical reactions and nanoscale phenomena. Throughout this series, we'll explore the fundamentals of DFT, its practical implementation, and its diverse applications. Whether you're a novice or an expert, join us on this journey through the fascinating world of Density Functional Theory simulation!

Density functional theory:

Density functional theory (DFT) is a quantum-mechanical atomistic simulation method to compute a wide variety of properties of almost any kind of atomic system: molecules, crystals, surfaces, and even electronic devices when combined with non-equilibrium Green's functions (NEGF).[1]

INTRODUCTION
DFT belongs to the family of first principles (ab initio) methods, so named because they can predict material properties for unknown systems without any experimental input. Among these, DFT has earned popularity due to the relatively low computational effort required.[1]
Using this theory, the properties of a many-electron system can be determined by using functionals, i.e. functions of another function. In the case of DFT, these are functionals of the spatially dependent electron density. (Electronic density is the measure of the probability of an electron being present at an infinitesimal element of space surrounding any given point. It is a scalar quantity depending upon three spatial variables and is typically denoted as either p(r)or n(r). The density is determined, through definition, by the normalised N-electron wavefunction which itself depends upon variables ( spatial and spin coordinates). Conversely, the density determines the wave function modulo up to a phase factor, providing the formal foundation of density functional theory. According to quantum mechanics, due to the uncertainty principle on an atomic scale the exact location of an electron cannot be predicted, only the probability of its being at a given position; therefore electrons in atoms and molecules act as if they are "smeared out" in space. For one-electron systems, the electron density at any point is proportional to the square magnitude of the wavefunction.) DFT is among the most popular and versatile methods available in condensed- matter physics, computational physics, and computational chemistry.

Vasp and Vaspkit

Definition: The Vienna Ab initio Simulation Package (VASP) is a computer program for atomic scale materials modelling, e.g. electronic structure calculations and quantum-mechanical molecular dynamics, from first principles.
I know its very zigzag definition, so 1st let's make it simple... Like why we need it?

Behind the scene: VASP computes an approximate solution to the many-body Schrödinger equation, either within density functional theory (DFT), solving the Kohn-Sham equations, or within the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation, solving the Roothaan equations. Hybrid functionals that mix the Hartree-Fock approach with density functional theory are implemented as well.[2]
Uses:
1. Functionals(LDA, GGAs, metaGGAs, Hartree-Fock, Hartree-Fock/DFT hybrids),
2.Structure relaxation
3.Molecular dynamics
4.Magnetism
5. Green's function methods
6.First derivatives
7.Linear response to ionic displacements
8.Linear response to electric fieldsLinear response to electric fields
9. Optical properties

Funamental Idea of Vaspkit: VASPKIT aims at providing a powerful and user-friendly interface to perform high throughput analysis of various material properties from the raw calculated data using the widely-used VASP code. The program can be conveniently run under either the interactive user interface or command line mode.[3]

Advantage:

  • Efficient Post-processing: VASPkit streamlines the post-processing of VASP output files, expediting the analysis of DFT simulation results.
  • Enhanced Analysis Capabilities: It offers a wide range of functionalities tailored for material analyses, allowing for in-depth examination of electronic structure data and properties.
  • Versatile Toolkit: Researchers can leverage VASPkit's diverse tools to extract valuable insights into the behavior of materials under various conditions and environments.
  • User-friendly Interface: Its intuitive interface makes it accessible to users of all levels, from novice researchers to seasoned experts, facilitating efficient data interpretation.
  • Empowering Research: By simplifying the analysis process and providing comprehensive tools, VASPkit empowers researchers to derive meaningful conclusions and advance their understanding of materials through DFT simulations.
